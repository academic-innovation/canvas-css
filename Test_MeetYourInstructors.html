0.3 Meet Your Instructors

 

Trent D Buskirk

Adjunct Research Professor

Trent D. Buskirk, PhD, is the Novak Family Distinguished Professor of Data Science and Chair of the Applied Statistics and Operations Research Department at Bowling Green State University. Before joining BGSU, he served as Director of the Center for Survey Research at UMASS Boston and Vice President for Statistics and Methodology at the Marketing Systems Group (MSG). He was a tenured faculty member in the Department of Biostatistics at Saint Louis University's School of Public Health. A Fellow of the American Statistical Association, Dr. Buskirk’s research focuses on mobile and smartphone survey designs, methods for calibrating and weighting nonprobability samples, and the application of big data and machine learning techniques in health, social, and survey science design and analysis. His work has been published in leading journals such as Cancer, Health Services Research, Social Science Computer Review, Journal of Official Statistics, Public Opinion Quarterly, and the Journal of Survey Statistics and Methodology. He has also served as President of the Midwest Association for Public Opinion Research (2016), Conference Chair for AAPOR (2018), and as a member of the scientific committees for the BigSurv18 and BigSurv20 conferences. When he’s not immersed in surveys or machine learning, Trent enjoys playing an action-packed game of pickleball.


Jinseok Kim

 

Research Assistant Professor

Jinseok Kim, PhD, is a Research Assistant Professor in the Survey Research Center at the Institute for Social Research and also holds a courtesy appointment in the U-M School of Information. His research focuses on how data quality influences knowledge discovery from big scholarly data and on improving data quality control in digital libraries through machine learning. Dr. Kim has made significant contributions to developing innovative machine learning methods for author name disambiguation—one of the most challenging data curation problems in digital libraries. His approaches to stratifying name disambiguation procedures and automating large-scale data labeling through record linkage have been funded by the National Science Foundation. They are being applied in several other funded research projects. He earned his PhD from the School of Information Sciences at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign in 2017. Dr. Kim has published extensively on topics such as machine learning for author name disambiguation, the impact of data quality on research findings, and network measurements in computer and information science. He has also taught courses on text mining of social media data using natural language processing and network analysis.


James Wagner

 

Research Professor

James Wagner, PhD, is a Research Professor at the University of Michigan’s Survey Research Center (UM-SRC). His research focuses on nonresponse and methods for addressing it during data collection, with particular emphasis on the use of responsive and adaptive designs to control nonresponse. He has also developed statistical decision rules to support these design strategies. Dr. Wagner has published extensively in leading journals, including Public Opinion Quarterly, Statistics in Medicine, Journal of the Royal Statistical Society, and Journal of Official Statistics, among others. He is the co-author of the 2017 book Adaptive Survey Design. With more than 20 years of experience in sample design, Dr. Wagner has worked on a wide range of sampling projects and serves as an instructor in the Michigan Program in Survey and Data Science and the Joint Program in Survey Methodology, where he teaches courses on statistics, sampling, and methods for addressing nonresponse. He has also consulted for several federal statistical agencies on strategies to improve data quality.


Brady T. West

 

Research Associate Professor

Brady T. West, PhD, is a Research Associate Professor in the Survey Methodology Program within the Survey Research Center at the Institute for Social Research at the University of Michigan–Ann Arbor (U-M). He earned his PhD from the Michigan Program in Survey and Data Science in 2011, an MA in Applied Statistics from U-M’s Statistics Department in 2002—where he was recognized as an Outstanding First-Year Applied Master’s student—and a BS in Statistics with Highest Honors and Highest Distinction from the same department in 2001. His research interests include measurement error in auxiliary variables and survey paradata, selection bias in surveys, responsive and adaptive survey design, interviewer effects, total survey error, analysis of complex sample survey data, and multilevel regression models for clustered and longitudinal data. Dr. West has authored over 180 peer-reviewed journal articles in the fields of statistical and survey methodology, public health, and epidemiology. He is the lead author of Linear Mixed Models: A Practical Guide Using Statistical Software (Second Edition, Chapman & Hall/CRC Press, 2014), with a third edition forthcoming, and co-author of Applied Survey Data Analysis (Second Edition, Chapman & Hall, 2017). Additionally, he serves as one of the three lead faculty instructors for the “Statistics with Python” series. Dr. West resides in Dexter, Michigan, with his wife, Laura, and their children, Carter and Everleigh.
